Hypothesis Development

For the purpose of this project we have formulated 4 Hypothesis that are to be tested :

  1. Null Hypothesis (H-0): There is no statistically significant difference in the mean math scores between male and female students.
  2. Alternative Hypothesis (H-1): There is a statistically significant difference in the mean math scores between male and female students.
  3. Hypothesis 2: There is no significant difference in the mean reading scores between male and female students.
  4. Hypothesis 3: There is no significant difference in the mean writing scores between male and female students.

Rationale Behind Hypothesis

The rationale for this hypothesis is to formally test a commonly held belief and a frequent topic of educational research: whether a gender gap exists in academic subjects. By analyzing the data, we can move from anecdotal evidence to a data-driven conclusion for this specific student population. This investigation is a crucial first step in understanding potential disparities and can inform more targeted educational strategies if a difference is found.

Method of Hypothesis Testing

To test this hypothesis, the ideal statistical method is the independent two-sample t-test.

Analysis and Results

Mathematics: The analysis reveals a statistically significant difference in math scores between male and female students (0.001). On average, males scored higher.

Reading: A statistically significant difference was also found in reading scores (0.001), with females scoring higher.

Writing: Similarly, there is a statistically significant difference in writing scores (0.001), where females again scored higher.
